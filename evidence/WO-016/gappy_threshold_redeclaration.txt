── §3.3 THE CASE FOR RECORDED-GAPS, AND ITS LIMITS (honestly) ───────────────────────────────
CASE: it measures the thing Branch 5 exists to detect — the sampler being STARVED off its wake —
  and nothing else. It does not fire on per-cycle overhead (which is not starvation) nor on
  connection-down idle (which is not starvation), and — the property that motivated the whole
  re-ruling — it does NOT DRIFT toward spurious VOID as the capture lengthens: a 24 h capture at
  this health accrues recorded-gap time proportional to real stalls, not proportional to overhead
  or to accumulated resync time. 0.08 % ≪ 10 % here means the loop was genuinely not starved.
LIMIT (what it CANNOT see): a missed wake only registers when an inter-wake interval overruns by
  ≥ LAG_GAP_FACTOR. If the sampler were starved UNIFORMLY just UNDER that factor (every wake late
  by, say, 1.5× interval when the factor is 2×), recorded-gaps would read ~0 while the loop was
  pervasively mildly starved. So a figure three orders below its threshold is "excellent" ONLY if
  the complementary signal agrees; it could otherwise be INSENSITIVE. THE COMPLEMENT: the lag
  DISTRIBUTION (median 8.97 ms, p95 13.60 ms, elevated>100ms = 0.04 %) is sensitive to exactly
  that sub-factor pervasive lag and here confirms health. ⇒ RECOMMEND: VOID uses recorded-gaps;
  the SEPARATE "laggy?" question keeps the elevated-lag-distribution metric. Two questions, two
  metrics — neither laundered through the other, and together they cover both failure modes.

── §3.4 THREE COMPONENTS PER RULE 0.1j (for the proposed recorded-gaps VOID metric) ──────────
  PER-SAMPLE BOUND : a wake is MISSED when its inter-wake interval overruns by ≥ LAG_GAP_FACTOR ×
                     interval (the existing LagSampleRecord.gaps definition; LAG_GAP_FACTOR is a
                     declared constant — state its value at implementation).
  AGGREGATION WINDOW: the measurement window (this run: the 3600 s capture; at corpus scale, per
                     analysis window — declare the window length in the corpus WO).
  VERDICT FRACTION : VOID if Σ(recorded-gap durations) / window > F. PROPOSED F = 10 % (same
                     magnitude as today, now over the correct quantity). F is a DECLARED THRESHOLD
                     → the lead rules it (0.4 / 3.1); this states the form, not a ratified number.
  (Rule 0.1j is satisfied only when all three are stated; this proposal states all three.)
