THE TWO SHAPES
--------------
SHAPE A (0.1h): a test SUPPLIES what PRODUCTION is supposed to PRODUCE — it hand-feeds a
  precondition instead of driving production to create it. (S10: the fresh snapshot.)
SHAPE B (0.1i): a test STOPS AT A CALL BOUNDARY the production path continues through — it
  asserts INVOCATION and never verifies the EFFECT. (_reconnect: escalation fires, callee was
  `pass` — so "it was called" certified nothing.)

DISTINCTION APPLIED (to avoid false positives): feeding RAW VENUE FRAMES (snapshots, book
updates, QuoteUpdate — the EXTERNAL venue's output, which a unit test cannot drive) is the
legitimate fixture boundary, NOT Shape A. Shape A is feeding what OUR code should produce.
Likewise, asserting a producer's real OUTPUT (e.g. the resubscribe SEND) is an effect, not a
call boundary; only asserting "a method was invoked" with no effect check is Shape B.

HIT B1 — test_data_adapters.py::...reconnect... (lines 225, 230). ONE test.
  SHAPE: it mocks `adapter._reconnect = Mock()`, drives five REAL checksum failures through the
  production path, and asserts `_reconnect` was called on the 5th (not before). It STOPS at the
  call boundary — it does not verify the reconnect EFFECT.
  CROSS-REFERENCE WITH §1: `_reconnect` is NOT a stub (stub-lint: zero unexamined stubs; and
  `_reconnect` now sets `_pending_reconnect`, exercised in production). So this is not the live
  S13 configuration (call-assertion on a `pass` callee).
  DISPOSITION — NOT A FALSE-CONFIDENCE GAP. The EFFECT is fully covered elsewhere:
  test_reconnect_to_effect.py::test_five_real_failures_reconnect_and_emission_resumes drives the
  real transport and asserts the OBSERVABLE END STATE (fresh socket opened, failed socket closed,
  EMISSION RESUMES, streak cleared, flag consumed) — and it deliberately does NOT assert
  `_reconnect` was called ("that assertion already existed and certified nothing"). B1 is a
  legitimate UNIT test of the escalation TRIGGER (does 5 failures fire the escalation?), with the
  effect owned by the integration test. The two are the correct layered pair, not a gap.

No other Shape B occurrences. Every other call-related assertion in the suite asserts a real
producer OUTPUT (e.g. `sockets[1].sent == ["unsubscribe","subscribe"]` — the resubscribe SEND is
the effect), not a bare invocation.

HIT A1 — test_snapshot_recovery.py::test_checksum_failure_requests_resubscribe_and_emission_resumes.
  SHAPE: after driving a REAL checksum failure through production (asserts `_pending_resubscribe`
  is set — production REQUESTED the snapshot), it hand-calls `await adapter._maybe_resubscribe(ws)`
  and then feeds SNAPSHOT_FRAME as the recovery snapshot. Supplying the fresh snapshot is the S10
  shape.
  DISPOSITION — DECLARED FIXTURE LIMIT, not a hidden hand-feed, and effect-covered. Its docstring
  states plainly: "this feeds the recovery snapshot as Kraken would deliver it AFTER a resubscribe.
  Only the isolated live re-run confirms Kraken actually responds... The producer send is exercised
  here; the venue's response is not." The PRODUCTION TRIGGER (checksum failure -> request) is
  exercised, and the full close/reopen/resubscribe/resume EFFECT is independently covered by
  test_reconnect_to_effect.py. The remaining un-driven step (the venue answering a resubscribe with
  a snapshot) is EXTERNAL — the legitimate fixture boundary — and is explicitly labelled. Not a gap.

EXAMINED AND REJECTED as Shape A (feeding EXTERNAL venue output or using a real interface):
  - set_market_state(...) in test_cost_reconciliation / test_double_count_bite_proof /
    test_preservation_guarantees / test_staleness_guard_bite_proof: `set_market_state` is the paper
    venue's REAL interface for receiving market data (the live loop calls it in production). Feeding
    a MarketState to test execution-layer behavior in isolation is proper layering; MarketState
    PRODUCTION is covered by the data-layer tests. The staleness tests feed a deliberately stale/
    absent MarketState to TRIGGER a guard — testing the guard, not hand-feeding a precondition.
  - test_live_loop.py::...pass_through (counters): feeds RAW book frames/QuoteUpdate — the venue's
    external output — to test raw-vs-emitted counting. Legitimate fixture input, not Shape A.
