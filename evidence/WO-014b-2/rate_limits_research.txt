NOT be confirmed verbatim on an official Kraken page in this pass. Per 0.1e it is
therefore treated as UNCITED — it may inform a conservative choice but MUST NOT be
presented as a Kraken citation. This is DOCUMENTED SILENCE (weaker than documented

PROPOSED FIGURES — DECLARED ENGINEERING JUDGMENT (for the implementation session)
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Anchored to what IS documented (reconnecting "too often" is penalized; limits are
load-dependent) and to the conservative, unconfirmed ~150/10min ceiling:

  * Exponential backoff with full jitter between reopen attempts:
      base 1s, factor 2 (1,2,4,8,16,...), CAP 30s, jitter = random in [0, current].
      Reasoning: 30s cap keeps a recovered venue's reconnect latency bounded while
      spacing attempts so a sustained outage cannot approach a ~150/10min ceiling.
      Even at the 30s cap that is 20 attempts / 10 min << 150 — an order of magnitude
      of headroom against the unconfirmed figure, which is the right posture when the
      limit is silent.
  * Circuit breaker: max 10 reopen attempts per rolling 10-minute window. On trip ->
      FAIL LOUD with a declared reason code and STOP the capture (Ruling B; pending-veto
      STOP semantics). Reasoning: 10/10min tolerates ordinary venue hiccups and the
      anticipated ~116 keepalive reconnects/24h (~0.8/10min average) with wide margin,
      while still bounding a pathological storm far below the conservative ceiling.

  ALL FIGURES ABOVE ARE ENGINEERING JUDGMENT under documented silence, to be stated as
  such in the backoff code's docstring — not as a Kraken citation.
