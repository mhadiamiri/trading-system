--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ONLY STATED — it is prose, not a property.
  - The convention is stated once, as a docstring line: src/trading/logkit/decision.py:10
    ("Valid Reason Codes (LAYER_VERB_DETAIL vocabulary)").
  - NO test enforces conformance. tests/test_reason_code_vocabulary.py enforces the four
    properties (raised=>declared, declared=>producible, prefix-freedom, scan-reads-emitted)
    — none checks the LAYER_VERB_DETAIL shape. Grep for a naming/pattern check across
    tests/ and src/ finds only the unrelated redaction-pattern-name test. Confirmed: the
    convention is unchecked.
  - EVIDENCE IT IS UNENFORCED: the declared strings visibly violate it. The layer lives in
    the DICT KEY, not always in the string — many codes carry no layer prefix:
      NO_SIGNAL, LONG_SIGNAL, SHORT_SIGNAL   (STRATEGY group, no STRATEGY_/STRAT_ prefix)
      ORDER_FILLED, ORDER_REJECTED           (EXECUTION group, no EXEC_ prefix — yet
                                              EXEC_ORDER_FILLED in the same group HAS it)
      CHECKSUM_RESYNC, HEARTBEAT_ABSENCE, PASS, CLAMP, VETO  (no layer token at all)
    A convention this unevenly followed is definitionally unenforced.
  - COST TO ENFORCE (not done, per instruction): this is NOT a test-add — it is a
    migration. Enforcing needs (1) a DECLARED grammar (allowed layer prefixes per group +
    allowed verbs), (2) a property asserting each code matches, and (3) RENAMING every
    non-conforming declared string above — renames of live audit vocabulary, which is
    exactly the taxonomy-migration WO's job and collides with its ORDER_FILLED/PASS/CLAMP/
    VETO moves. So the cost is "one property + a full vocabulary migration," best carried
    by that successor WO, not bolted on here.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
ALL FOUR lowercase feed event_types are STRINGS WE CHOSE (not transmitted representation).
They therefore NORMALIZE to uppercase per the lead's ruling — the FR-018a(f)
"transmitted-representation" exception does NOT apply.

Per value, with source (all are event_type="..." LITERALS in our own adapter, emitted from
OUR control-flow conditions, not read from any Kraken payload field):
  feed_connected     src/trading/data/adapters/kraken_public.py:114
        emitted by US after a successful connect+subscribe; paired reason_code=FEED_CONNECTED.
  payload_error      kraken_public.py:141 and :150
        emitted inside OUR `except json.JSONDecodeError` (:141, reason FEED_MALFORMED_PAYLOAD)
        and OUR generic `except Exception` (:150, reason FEED_UNEXPECTED_PAYLOAD).
  feed_disconnected  kraken_public.py:160
        emitted inside OUR `except ConnectionClosedError`; paired reason FEED_CONNECTION_CLOSED.
  feed_error         kraken_public.py:178
        emitted inside OUR generic connection `except Exception`; paired reason FEED_CONNECTION_ERROR.

WHY THEY ARE OURS, NOT KRAKEN'S:
  - Kraken v2 does not transmit these tokens. Its envelope uses channel/method/type values
    (status, heartbeat, subscribe, book, trade, ...). "feed_connected" / "payload_error" /
    "feed_disconnected" / "feed_error" describe OUR feed-lifecycle state, not a wire field.
  - Each is emitted at a site keyed to OUR condition (our connect success, our parse
    exception, our disconnect handler) — no value is copied out of an inbound message.
  - Each is ALREADY paired with an uppercase reason_code for the same fact
    (FEED_CONNECTED, FEED_MALFORMED_PAYLOAD, FEED_CONNECTION_CLOSED, FEED_CONNECTION_ERROR).
    The same fact already has an uppercase form in the adjacent namespace — corroboration
    the lowercase form is a stylistic choice of ours, not a transmitted token.
  Conclusion for item (4) of the taxonomy ruling: normalize all four to uppercase.
